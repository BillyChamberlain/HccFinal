Middle Ages

Renaissance

Eventually, with the rise of scholasticism, Europe entered into an age of reason and a rebirth of the arts, signalling an end to the Dark Ages. However, although new reasons allowed for better understanding of God’s word and creation, it also bred doubt. New philosophies aimed to pull people away from the Church. These “isms” became enemies of Church teaching. The Renaissance Popes were deeply entwined with the monarchy and engaged in all sorts of simony, selling church positions and indulgences (“tickets to heaven”).
